Write a great project description

Communicating effectively about your project streamlines the process.

A clear, detailed, and concise description of your research is a powerful tool to create opportunities and facilitate collaboration with others. In fact, documentation plays a critical role in data management.

When you craft your project description for dataCoLAB, certain key pieces of information will streamline the process and help us more quickly and effectively assist you. Depending on the stage of your research, you may be more certain about some of these categories than others. Clearly indicating those areas of uncertainty can also help us help you!

The most important pieces of information we need:

  • Research question. What is the goal of your data collection and analysis?
  • Current stage of research and data collection. Are you early in the process or well on your way?
  • Type. What kind of data are you working with (e.g., spreadsheet, images, code, text, numeric)?
  • Format. In what format or structure is your data being collected, analyzed, and/or stored?
  • Volume and complexity. How large is your data set? How many variables or observations are included?
  • Concerns about quality, accuracy, or uncertainty. For example, your data might have missing values, inconsistencies, outliers, or be affected by bias.
  • Type of analysis. How have you analyzed your data, or how do you plan to analyze it?

Want a little more help to put your project description together? Come by the dataCoLAB Open Hours every Wednesday from 1:00pm-3:00pm. Open Hours are held in “The Den” at the Roger Sorrells Engineering & Science Library.
